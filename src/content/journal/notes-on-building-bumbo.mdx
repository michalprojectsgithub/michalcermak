Bumbo started because I was tired of my own todo list.

Not the concept of a todo list — I find the concept entirely reasonable. I was tired of the specific texture of every todo app I tried. The badges. The streak counts. The "you're on a roll!" notifications. The ambient sense that you are always behind, always owing something to the system.

I wanted something quieter.

## What Bumbo is

Bumbo is a small Electron app. It lives in your menu bar. When you open it, you see a single day. A short list of things. A text area where you can write a note for yourself. Nothing syncs. Nothing notifies you. There are no priorities, no labels, no recurring tasks.

When the day ends, it ends. You can look back at what you did, but you can't be haunted by what you didn't finish. It just shows you today.

## What I learned building it

**Electron is heavier than it should be** for something this small. The executable is too large for an app whose entire state fits in a JSON file. I've accepted this as the price of desktop-native behaviour without learning Swift or C++.

**Calm software is an editorial problem.** The hardest decisions weren't technical — they were about what to leave out. Every feature I considered and rejected was reasonable in isolation. Recurring tasks: useful. Cloud sync: useful. Keyboard shortcuts for every action: useful. But each one would have added a small amount of ambient complexity. And ambient complexity accumulates into exactly the kind of app I was trying not to build.

**Building for yourself is freeing.** I shipped Bumbo when it worked for me, not when it was ready for anyone else. That's a luxury I won't always have, and I've tried to appreciate it.
